Cone Crusher Working Principle

Component Assembly Detail

Role in Crushing Circuit: Cone crushers work downstream of primary jaw crushers, refining pre-crushed material into finished products. They handle feed sizes typically under 450mm and produce output ranging from 6-51mm with reduction ratios of 3.5:1 to 8:1, depending on the application and crushing stage.

How It Works: Cone crushers operate through gyration and compression between a rotating mantle (moving component) and a stationary concave (fixed outer bowl). Material is fed into the top and crushed progressively as it moves downward through the crushing chamber. The continuous gyrating motion produces a high content of cubical material ideal for concrete and asphalt applications.

Key Applications: Ideal for producing sized aggregates (10mm, 20mm, 40mm) for construction, manufactured sand for building materials, and processing hard abrasive materials including gold, copper, iron, and diamond ores. Essential for mining, aggregate production, quarrying, and sand manufacturing operations requiring consistent product quality and high throughput.
