Schematic Diagram

Operation View

Role in Processing Circuit: Screw classifiers provide closed-circuit wet classification, separating slimes (fine material) from sandy (coarse) material. They excel at sizing and washing applications, dewatering sand or crushed material, and separating mud from sand in mineral processing and aggregate operations. Essential for producing clean, dewatered sand products.

How It Works: Slurry enters the inclined trough at the lower end. Fine particles remain suspended in the water and overflow the weir at the upper end (overflow product). Coarse sand settles to the bottom where a large rotating screw auger mechanically conveys it up the incline. As material travels upward, continuous dewatering occurs with water draining back through the settled bed. Dewatered sand discharges at the upper end.

Key Applications: Ideal for sand washing plants, aggregate dewatering, mineral processing classification, and separating coarse and fine materials. Processing capacity ranges from 30-240 m続/h depending on model size. Available in 15 models total: 10 single screw and 5 dual screw configurations. Screw diameter 0.5-2 meters, motor power 5-50 kilowatts.
