Spiral Classifier

Gravity Separation for Ore Classification and Dewatering

High Classification Efficiency (90%)
Closed-Circuit with Ball Mills
Capacity 30-240 m³/h

Schematic Diagram

Operation View

Role in Processing Circuit: Spiral classifiers form closed-circuit cycles with ball mills for efficient ore grinding and classification. They separate coarse particles from fine mud in gravity concentration plants, performing desliming and dewatering operations in metal beneficiation processes. Essential for separating materials by size and density in mineral processing applications.

How It Works: Slurry enters the inclined tank where gravity separates particles by size and density. Fine particles remain suspended in overflow water and discharge over the weir (overflow product). Coarse particles settle to the bottom where a rotating spiral screw mechanically conveys them upward along the incline to discharge (underflow product). The spiral provides continuous dewatering as material travels upward.

Key Applications: Ideal for closed circuits with rod mills and ball mills in ore grinding operations. High Weir Type handles coarser classification (0.83-0.15mm overflow), while Submerged Type processes finer material (0.15-0.07mm overflow). Achieves about 90% material recovery with stable classification efficiency. Available in single and dual screw configurations.
