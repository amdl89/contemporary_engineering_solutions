Roll Crusher

Compression Crushing for Soft to Medium-Hard Materials

Low Power Consumption (15-25% less)
Handles Wet and Sticky Feeds
Capacity up to 14,000 TPH

Schematic Diagram

Operation View

Role in Processing Circuit: Roll crushers provide secondary or tertiary compression crushing for soft to medium-hard materials. They excel at processing ROM coal with refuse, limestone, gypsum, trona, shale, bauxite, oil shale, clean coal, coke, salt, and quicklime. Particularly effective for materials with crushing strength up to 150-160 MPa.

How It Works: Material feeds between two parallel cylindrical rolls rotating in opposite directions. The gap between rolls determines product size. As material enters, compression forces crush it between the roll surfaces. Counter-rotating rolls pull material through while crushing, producing cubical product with minimal fines. Electric motors drive rolls via V-belt transmission.

Key Applications: Ideal for coal preparation, mineral processing, cement production, and handling difficult wet, sticky, or frozen feeds. Provides 4:1 reduction ratio with throughput from 5 to 14,000 TPH depending on model. Significantly lower dust and noise generation compared to hammer mills or impact crushers.

Compression action generates significantly less dust and noise pollution than hammer mills or impactors.

Built-In Overload Protection

Hydraulic or spring systems allow rolls to separate when uncrushable material passes, preventing damage.
